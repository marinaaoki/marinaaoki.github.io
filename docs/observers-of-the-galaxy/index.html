Observers of the Galaxy

Posted on March 5, 2021

Although I wrote this song, my memories of the entire process are quite vague. Perhaps because shortly afterwards, I performed in front of a crowd for the very first time singing this song and that whole experience was so overpowering that the songwriting process was overshadowed next to it. I approached my piano teacher at the time who helped me with the piano accomponiment. I owe a lot to her. I ended up playing it at a concert my grade organised in high school. We performed a lot of different pieces, including another piece I had written that my choir teacher had wrote the arrangement for.

I was so nervous when I stepped up to the piano alone. I played it two nights in a row. I still remember messing up one of the parts on the second night, but this is not necessarily a bad memory for me. It made me realise that songwriting is something I really enjoy, while performing is nice, but generally unpleasant due to the anxiety and fear that comes along with it. In the end, I was just immensely proud of myself.

I was born in November, so that is what is being referred to when I mention ‘winter bones’. I think this song was meant to represent how I often felt like an outsider looking in, just constantly watching events unfold as a passive observer never taking any action myself. That was a big theme for me during my final years at high school. I didn’t really do much, just let myself be pulled and carried by the flow. I didn’t actively organise any events or meetups, I was asked (or more often than not, just heard about these events after the fact). But I was still hopeful that things would get better and I was certain I wasn’t alone in feeling lonely. I’m still sure of that to this day.
